Boop

Players: 2
Board: 6×6 grid
Pieces per player: 8 kittens (no cats at the start)

# Objective

Win by either:
* forming a line of 3 of your cats (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal), or
* being the first to promote all 8 of your kittens into cats

# Setup

Each player takes 8 kittens of one color. The board starts empty. Players alternate turns.

# Turn

On your turn:
* Place one of your pieces (initially always a kitten) on any empty square.
* After placing, the piece “boops” all adjacent pieces (in the 8 surrounding squares):
  * Each adjacent piece is pushed one square directly away from the placed piece.
  * If a piece is pushed off the board, it returns to its owner’s supply.

# Piece behavior

* Kittens can be booped by any piece.
* Cats can only be booped by other cats; kittens cannot move them.

# Promotion

If you form a line of 3 of your kittens, those kittens are removed from the board and replaced by 3 cats in your supply.
If there are more than 3 kittens in a line, choose any alignment of 3 kittens to promote.
If all your 8 pieces are on the board, you may graduate any one kitten to a cat into your supply.
If a line of 3 is formed by both kittens and cats, kittens are promoted to cats and all three go to the supply.

Cats are then placed on future turns like normal pieces.

# Winning conditions

You win at the end of your turn if you formed a line of 3 cats, or if you have promoted all 8 of your kittens into cats.

In both cases, it doesn't matter if your opponent also has a winning position; you win immediately.

# Additional notes

Booping affects both players’ pieces, including your own.

Multiple pieces can be pushed in a single turn.

Chain reactions do not occur; each piece is pushed at most one square per placement.
